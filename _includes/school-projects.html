Academic Projects

  • Biomimetic Dexterous Hand - Winter 2025

    Biomimetic cable-driven dexterous hand is my winter 2025 project work-in-progress.

    I've always been fascinated by the complexity and dexterity of the human hand, one of our most remarkable tools. In this project, I'm designing a biomimetic hand that mimics human dexterity, using cable-driven phalangeal joints to minimize distal inertia.

  • Apex Putter - Embedded Systems in Robotics

    Apex Putter - robotic mini-golf is my final project submission to ME495 Embedded Systems in Robotics in Fall 2024.

    For this project, I integrated the concepts of robot calibration, kinematic control, computer vision, and machine learning, and made a Franka Emika Research robot play the game of putt-putt with any arbitrary ball position to hole-in-one.

    Project Repo

  • Grab-A-Pen Challenge - Northwestern MS Robotics Hackathon

    Grab-A-Pen is the final challenge in the MSR program orientation hackathon.

    In this challenge, I opitcally calibrated and registered a Trosen Robotics PincherX100 robot arm using an RGBD camera. The arm was then tasked to locate and grasp a pen and pass it along to other robot arms (video courtesy of Zhengxiao Han).

  • Mobile Manipulation - Robotic Manipulation

    Mobile Manipulation is the final project for ME449 Robot Manipulation.

    For this project, I integrated the concepts of robotic manipulation, forward kinematics, mobile odometry and linear control, and created a simulation of a kuka youBot picking a box up and placing it down in another location with CoppeliaSim.
