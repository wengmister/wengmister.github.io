Academic Projects

  • Biomimetic Dexterous Hand - Winter 2025

    Biomimetic Cable-Driven Dexterous Hand is my winter 2025 project work-in-progress.

    I've always been fascinated by the complexity and dexterity of the human hand, one of our most remarkable tools. In this project, I'm designing a biomimetic hand that replicates human dexterity using cable-driven phalangeal joints to minimize distal inertia.

  • Apex Putter - Embedded Systems in Robotics

    Apex Putter: Robotic Mini-Golf was my final project for ME495: Embedded Systems in Robotics in Fall 2024.

    For this project, I combined robot calibration, kinematic control, computer vision, and machine learning to enable a Franka Emika Research robot to play putt-putt. The robot could achieve a hole-in-one from any arbitrary ball position.

  • Grab-A-Pen Challenge - Northwestern MSR Hackathon

    Grab-A-Pen was the final challenge in the MSR program orientation hackathon.

    For this challenge, I optically calibrated and registered a Trosen Robotics PincherX100 robot arm using an RGBD camera. The arm was then programmed to locate and grasp a pen and pass it to other robot arms. (Video courtesy of Zhengxiao Han.)

  • Mobile Manipulation - Robotic Manipulation

    Mobile Manipulation was the final project for ME449: Robot Manipulation.

    For this project, I applied concepts from the course, including robotic manipulation, forward kinematics, mobile odometry, and linear control, to create a simulation in CoppeliaSim. In the simulation, a KUKA youBot picks up a box and places it in another location.

  • Jack-in-a-cup Simulation - Dynamics

    The Jack-in-a-Cup simulation serves as the final project for ME314: Dynamics.

    For this simulation, I applied Hamilton's Principles, a key concept in analytical mechanics, to model and analyze the motion of a jack interacting with a spinning box. It incorporates the effects of impacts and energy transfer as the jack repeatedly collides with the box walls while spinning. By solving the equations of motion, the simulation provides insights into the behavior of dynamic systems with constraints and collisions.
